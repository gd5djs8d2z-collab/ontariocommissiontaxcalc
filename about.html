About This Calculator

What this calculator does, where the rates come from, and what to expect

What This Calculator Does

The Ontario Commission Tax Calculator estimates the income tax, CPP, and EI withholding that applies to a single commission payment for an Ontario employee in 2026. It shows the marginal withholding impact of one commission payment when combined with your annual salary and annualised across your pay frequency. Everything runs in your browser — no data is sent anywhere or stored.

The calculator supports two modes:

  • Mode A (No TD1X): Withholding is calculated on the full gross commission — this is the CRA default for most commission earners.
  • Mode B (TD1X filed): Income tax withholding is reduced by employment expenses you have claimed on Form TD1X. CPP1, CPP2, and EI always apply to gross commission regardless of mode.

Data Sources — 2026 Rates

All rates come from CRA publications for the 2026 tax year:

  • Federal tax: five brackets from 14% to 33%; Basic Personal Amount of $16,452 (phased out between $181,440 and $258,482)
  • Ontario tax: five brackets from 5.05% to 13.16%; Basic Personal Amount of $12,989; surtax of 20% above $5,818 and 36% above $7,446
  • CPP: CPP1 at 5.95% up to YMPE $74,600 (max $4,230.45); CPP2 at 4.00% between $74,600 and YAMPE $85,000 (max $416.00)
  • EI: 1.63% on insurable earnings up to $68,900 (max $1,123.07)

Ontario Health Premium is not included in any calculations.

What Is Not Included

  • Ontario Health Premium
  • Quebec-specific deductions
  • Employer CPP/EI matching contributions
  • Non-refundable tax credits other than the Basic Personal Amount
  • RRSP, pension, or other deductions from income

Estimates Only — Not Tax Advice

Results shown are payroll withholding estimates, not what you will ultimately owe CRA. Commission withholding often runs higher than actual year-end tax because the annualisation formula treats each payment as if it repeats all year. Any excess comes back as a refund when you file your T1. Do not rely on these numbers as tax advice — consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
